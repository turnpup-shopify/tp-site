Soy-Glazed Salmon Bowls

Recipe by: Kendra Vaculin
Servings: 4

Ingredients

  • 1" piece ginger, peeled, finely grated
  • 1 garlic clove, finely grated
  • ¼ cup unseasoned rice vinegar
  • 2 Tbsp. extra-virgin olive oil, divided
  • 1 tsp. Diamond Crystal or ½ tsp. Morton kosher salt, plus more
  • 12 oz. green beans, trimmed, halved crosswise
  • 1 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 4 6-oz. skinless salmon fillets or one 1–1¼-lb. skinless salmon fillet, cut into 1" pieces
  • 2 Tbsp. soy sauce
  • 2 Tbsp. honey
  • Steamed jasmine rice and sesame seeds (for serving)

Instructions

  1. Whisk ginger, garlic, vinegar, 2 Tbsp. oil, and 1 tsp. Diamond Crystal or ½ tsp. Morton kosher salt in a large bowl; set dressing aside.
  2. Cook beans in a medium pot of boiling salted water until crisp-tender, about 2 minutes. Drain; transfer to reserved dressing. Add tomatoes and let sit, stirring occasionally.
  3. Pat salmon dry and place in a medium bowl. Season with salt; toss to coat. Stir soy sauce and honey in a small bowl. Heat remaining 1 Tbsp. oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high. Cook salmon in a single layer, undisturbed, until edges are opaque, about 2 minutes. Add soy sauce mixture; cook, tossing often, until salmon is coated and cooked through, 2–3 minutes.
  4. Divide rice among plates; top with salmon and glaze. Nestle beans and tomatoes alongside, spooning dressing over. Sprinkle with sesame seeds.
